Brazil

Experience the vibrant culture, stunning landscapes, and infectious energy of South America's largest country

Back to Countries

Quick Facts

Capital: Brasília

Population: 213 million

Language: Portuguese

Currency: Brazilian Real (R$)

Best time to visit: December-March (for beaches), June-September (for wildlife)

Cultural Highlights

Brazil is known for its vibrant culture, with influences from Indigenous, African, and European traditions. The country is famous for its festivals, particularly Carnival, which features samba dancing, elaborate costumes, and street parties.

Brazilian cuisine varies by region but is known for its churrasco (barbecue), feijoada (black bean stew with meat), and açai bowls.

Travel Tips

Check visa requirements before traveling

Carry small bills and be cautious with valuables

Learn a few basic Portuguese phrases

The country is huge - focus on one or two regions per trip

Top Attractions

Christ the Redeemer, Rio de Janeiro

The iconic statue overlooking Rio from Corcovado Mountain, one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Iguazu Falls

One of the world's largest and most impressive waterfall systems, straddling the border between Brazil and Argentina.

Amazon Rainforest

The world's largest tropical rainforest, home to an incredible diversity of wildlife and indigenous communities.
